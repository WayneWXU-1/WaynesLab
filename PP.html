Earbud Holder

I got tired of putting my earbuds in my pocket and it becoming a tangeled mess within five seconds and untangling them, as a result I designed this earbud holder.
As a way to hold earbuds I produced a series of prototypes (lost them) and went through a design process to find the optimal size. My first one was similar to a donut and was too small to fit the entire wire, and my second one was structurally unstable and broke. I ended up with this two hole donut design, if I did use wires, this would ensure all that the wires were untangled and the structure was stable. This was made in sketchup then 3d printed

Waterhose Adapter

This is a prototype on Solidworks of a waterhose attachment that should make watering hoses for those with a hand impairment more accesible, our group designed several designs on solidworks, fortunatly a better model moved forward as was presented.

What it does

  • Attachs to the head of a standard commercial waterhose
  • A torque or rotation would be applied on one side of the system and since the screw is attached to the waterhose it would change the settings
  • Makes watering the garden accessible for everyone

How it works

  • By applying a torque on one side it would turn the settings on the hose
  • The attachment can be easily attached by removing then reattaching the screw
  • It would likely need to be water jet cutted out of some metal material to ensure structural stability

What I learned

  • Calling homedepot and various supply depots to find the dimensions of what I need, i.e screws, diameters, etc
  • Using the design process to find the best and most simple problem
  • This was the 2nd or 3rd time using solidworks, so I learned assembling parts and various different features.

What I would improve

  • Making it assemble properly was quite difficult
  • We didnt actually print this, however I would make sure that it actual fit the commercial waterhose
  • Produce more models to see physical strains and stress over time

"Prototype Storage of some sort"

This is my first fully individual ardiuno project, initially it was planned to make a garbage can but some difficulty with the hinge the project turned into a servo powered container

What it does

  • A servo motor rotates opening and closing the lid
  • A button press triggers the servo to open and close
  • Essentially a storage space for keys, usbs, small items

How it works

  • Using some jumper wires, arduino, button and breadboard
  • The servo rotates 180 degrees when button pressed and then 0 degrees when released
  • Cardboard was used to build the box
  • An extra peice of cardboard at orthoganal angle was placed to change the rotation on a single axis to rotate like a hinge

What I learned

  • Applying course knowledge into my own arduino design following the design process
  • Applying lab knowledge of circuit analysis
  • The importance of prototyping

What I would improve

  • Moving the hinge to the left or right, this will allow more storage
  • Using a different material like aluminum sheets
  • include other systems like a lock with password in the future
